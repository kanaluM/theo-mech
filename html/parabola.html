Free-fall projectile in 2D

Consider the case of a projectile in free fall in 2D. Suppose our boundary conditions are that the projectile travels from (0, 0) to (250, 160) in 1 second (parameterized from \(t=0\) to \(t=1\)). If we only consider some reasonable parabolic paths, we can ask exactly which path it will take. In other words, given a parameterized path of the form \[y(t)=y_0 + (v_0\sin\theta) t + 4.9t^2\] for what value of \(p\) will the projectile's path satisfy our boundary conditions? We want to minimize the action given by \[KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{1}{2}m(x'(t)^2+y'(t)^2)\] \[PE = mgy = mgy(t)\] \[S = \int_{0}^{1} KE-PE \ dt\] Though we could solve this integral by hand, we can also solve it computationally by varying \(p\) and trying to minimizing \(S\). Note that potential energy is graphed as \(-mgy\) since it is being subtracted in the Lagrangian (\(KE-PE\))
