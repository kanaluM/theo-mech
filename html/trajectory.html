Free-fall projectile in 1D

Consider the case of a projectile in free fall in 1D. Suppose our boundary conditions are that the projectile travels to and from (0, 0) in 2 seconds (parameterized from \(t=-1\) to \(t=1\)). If we only consider reasonable paths straight up or down, we can ask how high the projectile will go. In other words, given a parameterized path of the form \[y(t) = (1+p)(1-t^2)\] \[y'(t) = -2t(1+p)\] for what value of \(p\) will the projectile's path satisfy our boundary conditions? We want to minimize the action given by \[KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = 2mt^2(1+p)^2\] \[PE = mgy = mg(1+p)(1-t^2)\] \[S = \int_{-1}^{1} [KE-PE] \ dt= \int_{-1}^{1} 2mt^2(1+p)^2-mg(1+p)(1-t^2) \ dt\] Though we could solve this integral by hand, using the Euler-Lagrange Equation, \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}-\frac{d}{dx}\frac{\partial f}{\partial y'}=0\] we can also solve it computationally by varying \(p\) and trying to minimizing \(S\). Note that potential energy is graphed as \(-mgy\) since it is being subtracted in the Lagrangian (\(KE-PE\))
